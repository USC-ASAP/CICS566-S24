CSCI 566 Machine Learning 1: Mathematical Foundations

Prospective Students: The course website is still under construction; please check back then. Yue Zhao will be the instructor for this course (note the name is no longer Deep Learning and Its Applications).

Overview: The major objective of this course is to introduce the mathematical foundations of machine learning. Particular focus will be laid on the conceptual understanding of the math behind machine learning models and algorithms, their applications, and hands-on experience.

Prerequisites: (1) Undergraduate level training or coursework in linear algebra, multivariate calculus, basic probability and statistics; (2) Skills in programming with Python (self-studying numpy, scipy, scikit-learn, and related packages is expected).

Basic logistics:

  • Time: Fridays, 1:00pm-4:20pm PST
  • Location: THH 201
  • Discussion: Piazza
  • Contact: Students should use Piazza for any course-related questions. For external inquiries, personal matters, or emergencies, you can email the CSCI 566 staff at usc.csci566@gmail.com. Please do not email any of the CSCI 566 staff individually.
